Aurora Borealis

The Aurora Borealis, also referred to as the Northern Lights, is a captivating natural light display predominantly observed in high-latitude areas near the Arctic. This phenomenon arises from the interaction between solar wind and the Earth's magnetic field

Interesting Facts:

  • Most visible in regions near the Arctic Circle
  • Ideal viewing is during winter when nights are longest and darkest
  • The display can feature colors such as green, pink, red, yellow, blue, and violet."
  • Created by the collision of charged particles with atmospheric gases.

Common Viewing Areas:

Regions known for aurora sightings include:

  • Northern Europe
  • Subarctic regions of Canada
  • Remote areas of Alaska
  • Greenland and nearby islands
